# LinkedIn Content Plan — 2026

## Core Principle
The 360Brew algorithm rewards **genuine relevance** and **dwell time**, not tricks, timing, or pods.
Your content must be matched to a specific audience with a clear topic lane. The system needs a "content fingerprint" of you — give it one.

---

## Phase 0: Research Before Posting (72 Hours)
Before writing a single post:

1. Find 20–30 accounts in your niche with 5K–30K followers and strong engagement from your ICP (not follower count)
2. Study them like case studies: What topics do they return to? What hooks stop the scroll? What formats get saves?
3. Identify their **outliers** — what made those posts uniquely successful?
4. Map what's missing in your niche — what nobody is saying but everyone needs to hear
5. List 3–5 competitors' ICP accounts where you'll comment strategically

**You are learning the unwritten rules of your niche before you play.**

---

## The 4-Bucket Content Funnel

### Bucket 1 — Growth (35–40%)
Goal: reach new people who don't know you yet.

| Format | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Brandjacking** | Take a known brand's decision/campaign, use it as evidence for your own insight. You're not reporting — you're redirecting their attention toward your expertise. The Boomerang Effect: when the brand or person engages, distribution compounds. |
| **Newsjacking** | Be the first credible voice to *contextualise* (not summarise) a breaking story in your industry. Post within 24–48 hours. LinkedIn is always late — X is where stories break first. The test: can you answer "so what?" in one sentence? |
| **Namejacking** | Reference a specific person your ICP already follows. Add your genuine perspective — not a summary. When you reference the right person with a real take, sometimes they notice. When they engage, their whole audience sees it. |
| **Hot Takes** | A genuinely held belief that challenges industry consensus. Not controversy for clicks — a position that forces people to pick a side. Test: does it make you slightly nervous to post? If not, it's not contrarian enough. |

**These formats work by attaching your expertise to attention that already exists. You're walking into a conversation that's already happening.**

---

### Bucket 2 — Authority (30–35%)
Goal: turn attention into trust. Prove you're a practitioner, not a theorist.

- Deep frameworks you use with clients
- Specific case studies with real numbers
- Behind-the-scenes of how you solve a known problem
- Step-by-step breakdowns of your methodology
- "How I" content (see Writing Style below)

**Authority content alone (without Growth content first) = handing out a business card in an empty room.**

---

### Bucket 3 — Conversion (20%)
Goal: ask for the business. Only works after Growth + Authority have done their job.

- Lead magnet with a comment or DM CTA
- Announcing open client spots
- Newsletter or tool sign-up
- Specific offer with a direct link

If you've built trust, conversion posts feel natural — not desperate.

---

### Bucket 4 — Personal (10%)
Goal: make you memorable. The human behind the expertise.

- Real updates your community actually cares about
- Failures, pivots, lessons mid-journey
- Behind the scenes of what you're building
- Only works once you've earned attention through Buckets 1 & 2

**This is NOT "what my dog taught me about B2B sales."**

## The "How I" Writing Shift
2026 rule: Move from **How To** → **How I**

| How To (dying) | How I (works) |
|---|---|
| "5 steps to write a LinkedIn hook" | "I tested 47 hooks last month. Here's what I learned about my audience." |
| "Framework for client onboarding" | "We changed our onboarding process after losing a client. Here's what we do differently." |
| "How to price your services" | "I raised my prices 40% and lost two clients. Here's why I'd do it again." |

**Why:** AI can generate How To content instantly. Your lived experience, your failures, your real decisions — it can't.

How I content is also **AI-proof for citations** — LinkedIn is the #2 source in AI overviews. Generic tutorials get absorbed. Your specific story becomes a citable source.

**The positioning shift:** Move from **guru** (expert dispensing wisdom from a stage) to **guide** (fellow traveler figuring it out in real time alongside your audience). Counterintuitively, the guide positioning is more powerful right now — everyone is navigating a fast-changing landscape, and anyone claiming to have it all figured out sounds out of touch.

> "I'll be embodying the role of a teacher less in this season. Right now, I feel like I'm back to being a student, a friend on a parallel journey." — Jay Claus

Vulnerability is the mechanism. Admitting you're mid-process builds more trust than polished certainty.

## Technical Rules

### The 50-Word Rule
LinkedIn's retrieval system reads only the **first 60 tokens (~45–50 words)** to decide if your post enters the candidate pool at all.

- Your hook must signal: topic + audience in the opening line
- Setup, context, and warm-up can come after — but your first 50 words must have enough signal for an AI and a human to understand what the post is about and who it's for
- If the first 50 words could belong to anyone in your industry, they're too generic

### Write for Saves
- 1 save ≈ 5x the reach impact of 1 like
- Ask before publishing: "Would my ideal client bookmark this? Would they send it to a colleague?"
- Saves come from: frameworks, checklists, data, step-by-step breakdowns, reference content

### Dwell Time is the Primary Metric
The algorithm tracks whether people stopped and actually read your post — not just whether they liked it.
- Genuine > fast
- Tactical > motivational
- Specific > generic

### Visuals
Always include a visual if possible (image, one-pager, carousel, screenshot). Posts with visuals consistently outperform text-only.
- Name carousel files with keyword-rich descriptive titles before uploading
- Add alt text to all images

## Writing Process (5 Steps)
1. **Choose intent** — which bucket is this for? Shapes hook, structure, CTA
2. **Write the body first** — specific numbers, frameworks, examples. Get it all out
3. **Mine for the hook** — find the most surprising/provocative line in your body. Move it to the top
4. **Add a visual** — image, one-pager, screenshot, carousel
5. **Match the CTA to intent** — Growth: question that sparks discussion. Authority: "DM me for template". Conversion: direct link or keyword. Personal: often no CTA needed

**Hook must do one of:** create curiosity / challenge a belief / promise specific value

## Content Infrastructure

### Notion Content Calendar (Kanban)
- **Ideas** — raw half-formed thoughts, hooks, topics (keep 100+ here)
- **In Progress** — actively writing
- **Ready for Design** — copy done, needs visuals
- **Needs Edits** — content or design revisions
- **Posted** — all published posts tagged by format and date

Every card tagged by format: carousel / one-pager / video / image / text post

### Content North Stars
Before building your bank, define 5–10 guiding principles that every (or most) piece of content must align with. These are your north stars — the fixed direction you always move toward.

Example north stars:
- "I turn operational problems into system solutions"
- "I show what's actually working in B2B content, not what worked in 2022"
- "I prove that smaller accounts can outperform bigger ones with the right system"

Without north stars, your content goes in multiple directions, your audience gets confused, and content-market fit becomes nearly impossible to find. Post consistently toward the same destination.

Pair north stars with a **tone and voice guide** — this is what you upload to your AI writing tool so it understands your style. Include: banned words (e.g. leverage, unlock, game changer, dive deep), formatting rules (no em-dashes, 1–3 line paragraphs, line breaks between paragraphs), tone (conversational, use contractions), and examples of posts you love vs. AI slop to avoid.

### Content Bank Sources
- Sales call transcripts — every objection is a post, every question is a hook
- Internal SOPs and frameworks — obvious expertise that's invisible to the person who has it
- Conference presentations — extract 3 LinkedIn ideas from each
- Client conversations — patterns you see repeatedly = authority content

**You are not creating content. You are extracting what already exists.**

### AI Writing Tool Setup (Claude Project)
For AI-assisted writing, your Claude project needs:

**Instructions:**
- Claude's role in this project
- Your identity: who you are, what you do, who you help
- Your original story
- Your opinions — spicy takes, contrarian beliefs, things you hate in your industry
- Writing rules: banned words, formatting rules, tone
- Connected to Google Drive (so it has maximum context from your source material)

**Knowledge base (files to upload):**
- 5–10 of your best LinkedIn posts (teaches writing style)
- Call transcripts and client conversations
- Case studies and frameworks you use
- A "posts I love" doc with examples
- An "AI slop examples" doc — posts Claude should never produce

## Posting Schedule
- **Target:** 5x per week (Mon–Fri)
- **Minimum sustainable:** 3x per week
- **Best times for B2B:** 7–9am Eastern, Tuesday–Thursday (also Sunday mornings)
- **Consistency beats volume** — pick a cadence you can maintain for 6 months

## The Comment Flywheel (First 90 Days)
Your comments matter more than your posts when starting out.

**Why:** Commenting on a post with 50K impressions = borrowing that creator's audience. Your own posts to zero followers reach almost no one.

**How to comment well:**
- Add a new angle the original post didn't cover
- Respectfully challenge the take with nuance
- Tell a quick story that connects to the topic
- Never: "Great post!" — it does nothing

**Important distinction:** Your engagement with other people's posts does NOT affect whether your content reaches someone else. It only shapes what gets served in your own feed. Strategic commenting matters for visibility and relationship-building — not for boosting your own post distribution.

What it *does* do: the algorithm maintains a time-ordered sequence of every post you've positively engaged with and uses it to understand your professional interests. Engage heavily in your niche — you're training the system to show you the best thinking in your space, which sharpens what you create and surfaces the right people to connect with.

**Engagement routine:**
- 30 min strategic commenting BEFORE opening your drafts
- Comment on posts from creators whose audiences match your ICP — quality over virality
- Build a simple engagement list (spreadsheet): Creator name + Activity Feed URL (NOT profile URL — using profile URL means they see you visiting daily = creepy)
- Reply to every comment on your own posts within the first 2 hours

## What Stopped Working (Do Not Do)
- Engagement pods — the system tracks dwell time and knows the difference between a real comment and "Great insight!"
- Timing hacks — algorithm now embeds posts within 30 min and indexes within 1 min. Early velocity matters less
- Hashtag stuffing — 360Brew reads semantic meaning, not labels
- Generic advice — "5 tips for productivity" competes with 10,000 identical posts
- Posting and ghosting — not replying to comments wastes your best growth moment

## Newsjacking Automation (Advanced)
For staying ahead of news and generating post ideas daily:

**Level 1 — Claude Co-work** (simplest, runs on your laptop):
Prompt Claude to research the latest news in your space and draft 10 newsjacking LinkedIn posts. Schedule to run at 6am daily.

**Level 2 — n8n workflow** (more powerful, runs without you):
Scrapes X accounts + Google News → AI evaluates newsjacking potential → drafts posts → delivers to email or Slack every morning.

**Level 3 — OpenClaw agent** (fully autonomous):
AI agent running 24/7 on a server. Monitors web, drafts, delivers via Telegram without any trigger needed. Future-facing — treat like an employee who never sleeps.

**Key principle:** LinkedIn is always 24–48 hours behind X. Being first on LinkedIn means monitoring X.
Test for a good newsjacking post: "Can I answer 'so what?' in one sentence?" If not — keep going.

Do not quit before post 41.

## Smaller Account Advantage
LinkedIn's AB test data from the retrieval rebuild:
- Accounts with fewer connections saw a **3.29% revenue increase** and **1.17% increase in professional interactions** with the new algorithm
- A top-10% post from a 5–10K follower account gets ~5.5K impressions
- A median post from a 25–50K follower account gets ~2.4K impressions

**A great post from a small account outperforms an average post from an account 5x its size.**

If you've been waiting to build a bigger audience before going serious — stop waiting. The algorithm was rebuilt in your favor.
