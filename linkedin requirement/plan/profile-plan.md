# LinkedIn Profile Plan — 2026

## Objective
Turn your profile from a resume into a conversion funnel.
The algorithm's 360Brew/GPU-RAR system reads your full profile as a text prompt before distributing any of your content. A weak profile = weak distribution, regardless of post quality.

---

## How the Algorithm Uses Your Profile (Two Distinct Roles)
Your profile serves two different jobs in the algorithm — this distinction changes what you optimise for:

- **Author-side (content distribution):** Only 5 fields travel with your post into other people's feeds. These tell the AI who you are and which audience should see your content.
- **Viewer-side (feed curation):** Your full rich profile — skills, job history, certifications, languages — is used to understand what *you* want to see in your own feed. This does NOT affect whether your content reaches someone else.

Important: your engagement with other people's posts only shapes your own feed. It does not affect whether your content reaches someone else's feed.

---

## The 5 Author Data Fields (Critical)
These are the ONLY profile fields that travel with your post into other people's feeds. Optimise these first.

| Field | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Use your ICP's language — what they search for, not your job title |
| **Company Name** | Add a descriptor: `YourCompany → What it does` |
| **Title** | Use a functional title (e.g. "Fractional CMO"), not "Founder" or "Consultant" |
| **Industry** | Match to the topics you post about — mismatches confuse the AI |
| **Name** | Professional, searchable, consistent across platforms |

---

## Headline Formula
**[Result you deliver] for [ICP] | [Platform/Method] | [Optional: Credibility signal]**

Examples:
- `Helping B2B SaaS founders build content engines that generate inbound | LinkedIn + YouTube | Speaker`
- `SEO & AEO consultant for B2B SaaS | Organic + AI search | ex-Agency`
- `Building content systems for executives | Organic LinkedIn growth | Agency founder`

**Test:** If your ideal client saw your headline, would they immediately think "this person can help me"? If no — rewrite it.

## Banner
- Acts as a **re-hook** — the post got the click, now the banner must pull them further down
- Should communicate: what you do + who you help, visually
- Use consistent brand colour for recognition over time
- Avoid generic stock photos

---

## Profile Photo
- Clear, professional, approachable
- If you have a brand colour, use it as the background for visual consistency across content

---

## About Section Structure: Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
```
[Opening line that names the pain your ICP has]

[Your solution / how you solve it]

[Specific results / proof — use numbers]

[CTA — tell them exactly what to do next]
```

- Write it as a sales page, not an autobiography
- Use natural keywords your buyers search for (no stuffing)
- Keep it scannable — short paragraphs, no walls of text

---

## Featured Section
Priority order:
1. **Newsletter sign-up** or **booking link** (always first — you own the relationship off LinkedIn)
2. **Lead magnet** or downloadable resource
3. Only include a post if it genuinely showcases exceptional results

LinkedIn owns your followers. Featured section = the bridge to your ecosystem.

---

## Custom CTA Button
- LinkedIn lets you add a button directly on your profile
- Link it to your booking page, newsletter, or lead magnet — whatever action you want
- Most people leave this blank. Don't.

---

## LinkedIn SEO (Profile Optimisation)
LinkedIn is the #2 most cited source in AI overviews. Optimise for search:

- **Headline:** Include keywords buyers actually search for
- **About section:** Write naturally but with keywords your ICP uses
- **Services section:** Searchable gold — fill it with exact ICP language
- **Post consistently** around your core topic — the algorithm builds a "content fingerprint" over time
- **Image alt text:** Add keyword-friendly descriptions (almost nobody does this)
- **Carousel filenames:** Name files descriptively before uploading — the system reads it
- **Newsletters:** Rank on Google — treat them like blog posts, not just repurposed posts

## Profile Audit Checklist
- [ ] Headline uses ICP language, not your job title
- [ ] Company name carries meaningful context
- [ ] Title is functional and specific
- [ ] Industry matches your posting topics
- [ ] Banner communicates value proposition visually
- [ ] About section follows Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
- [ ] Featured section links to newsletter or booking (not random posts)
- [ ] Custom CTA button is activated and linked
- [ ] Services section is filled in with ICP keywords
- [ ] Image alt text added to all profile photos
- [ ] Stranger test passed: someone new can explain what you do within 10 seconds

---

## The Stranger Test
Send your profile URL to someone who doesn't know you.
**Ask:** "What do I do and who do I help?"
If they can't answer in 10 seconds — your profile is broken. Fix it before posting anything.
